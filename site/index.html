The Know-How

Why Sealing Works — and Why by Hand

A little straight talk so you know exactly what you're paying for.

Why seal at all?

Asphalt is held together by a tar-like binder that slowly dries out and breaks down under the sun's UV rays, rain, oil, gas, and Ontario's freeze-thaw winters. As it ages it fades to grey, turns brittle, and starts to crack. Sealing is the protective layer that slows all of that down and adds years to your driveway.

How it protects

A fresh coat seals out water and blocks damaging UV, and fills the tiny surface voids where cracks begin. Water can't soak in and freeze, and oil and gas can't eat away at the surface — so your driveway stays flexible, deep black, and intact far longer.

Why we squeegee by hand

Spraying is fast, but it lays down a thin, surface-level coat that can miss spots. Applying by hand with a squeegee forces the sealer down into the pores and cracks, building a thicker, even, better-bonded coat. That's the difference between a seal that lasts and one that wears off in a season.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does it cost?

Every driveway is different, so we give free, no-pressure quotes based on size and condition. Call or text Marcus at (416) 474-8131 for yours.

How long does the sealing last?

With commercial-grade sealer applied by hand, you'll get years of protection. Resealing every 2–3 years keeps your asphalt in top shape — and we'll tell you honestly when it's due.

How soon can I drive on it?

Plan to stay off a freshly sealed driveway for about 24–48 hours so it fully cures. We'll give you exact timing on the day, based on the weather.

Do you repair cracks too?

Yes. We fill and prep hairline and worn cracks before sealing, so small problems don't grow into expensive ones.

What's the best time of year?

Sealing is done in warm, dry weather — typically spring through fall in Ontario — so the sealer can cure properly.
