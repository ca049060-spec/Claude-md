Why the rebuild matters

The clutch washers wear, the clutch slips — and if ignored, debris can reach the engine.

Not optional: worn clutch washers let the clutch slip and can shed metal into the engine — a ~$450 job that prevents a new-motor bill. Plastic bearing cages also go brittle with heat over time (the "2-year" half of the rule).

07 Step-by-step rebuild

Start to finish

You know this work, Arman — this is just the full sequence on record, with the key torque call-outs.

A
Remove the supercharger
1
Disconnect the battery
Negative first. Pull the seats / rear access panel to reach the engine bay.
2
Clear access
Remove the air intake / silencer and any covers over the PTO (clutch) side where the supercharger sits.
3
Free the charge pipe
Loosen the clamp and pull the intercooler / charge hose off the supercharger outlet.
4
Unbolt & pull the S/C
Remove the retaining bolts and slide the supercharger off its splined drive. Cap the opening so nothing drops in.
B
Disassemble on the bench
5
Secure it
Mount the supercharger in the vise (use a support plate / soft jaws). Fit the shaft-holder tool.
6
Remove the cap nut
Hold the shaft, crack the cap nut. (Reassembly spec: 22 ft-lb.)
Cap nut · 22 ft-lb
7
Pull the clutch washer stack
Remove the washers in order — note orientation. This is the main wear item being renewed.
8
Off with the drive gear & bearings
Fit the gear-holder, remove the drive gear, then press out the old bearings and seals.
C
Rebuild
9
Clean everything
Solvent-clean the housing and impeller. Inspect the volute and shaft bore for scoring.
10
Press in new bearings & seals
From the kit: new bearings (×2), seals, o-ring. Press squarely, fully seated.
11
New washers — with IsoFlex grease
Assemble the new clutch washers in the correct order, greased with IsoFlex Topas NB 52. Fit the new shaft & drive gear.
Grease: IsoFlex Topas NB 52 only
D
Torque & verify
12
Torque the cap nut
Hold the shaft and torque the cap nut to spec.
Cap nut · 22 ft-lb
13
Check clutch slip torque
Confirm the rebuilt clutch slips within spec — not too loose, not seized. This is the quality check that protects the engine.
E
Reinstall & test
14
Refit the supercharger
New o-ring, slide onto the splined drive, torque the mounting bolts (per shop-manual spec), reconnect the charge pipe & intake.
15
Oil change & battery
Fresh XPS 4-stroke oil + filter is the right move while you're in here. Reconnect the battery.
16
Run it & confirm boost
Start, check for leaks, confirm it pulls boost and holds. A quick water test and it's done.

Done = supercharger rebuilt with new washers/bearings/seals, cap nut at 22 ft-lb, slip torque verified, back on the ski, fresh oil, holding boost. Clean and quiet.
