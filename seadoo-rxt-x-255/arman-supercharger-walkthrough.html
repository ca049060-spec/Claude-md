THE JOB CH 4 / 10
The one thing it needs

Supercharger rebuild

100-HOUR SERVICE · DUE NOW

At 100 hours (or 2 years) the supercharger gets a rebuild: new clutch washers, bearings, seals, and shaft hardware from the kit.

Why it's not optional: as the clutch washers wear, the clutch starts to slip. Left too long, it can shed metal/debris into the engine — which turns a ~$400 job into a new motor. The plastic bearing cages also get brittle with heat cycles over time, which is the "2-year" half of the rule.

Bottom line: it's the single most important scheduled service on these skis — and mine's right at the line.

THE WORK CH 7 / 10
What the job looks like

The rebuild, start to finish

You know this cold — just so we're on the same page:

  • Pull the supercharger off the engine
  • Strip it down on the bench — clutch, bearings, seals, shaft
  • Press in the new bearings, fit the new washers & shaft from the kit
  • Reassemble to spec with blue threadlocker, torque the hardware
  • Reinstall on the engine, reconnect, and run it to confirm it boosts & holds

Done = S/C rebuilt to torque spec, back on the ski, fired up, holding boost. Clean and quiet.
